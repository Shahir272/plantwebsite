The Jewel of the Garden

Strawberries are the quintessential home garden fruit. They are incredibly versatile, fitting into almost any garden space‚Äîfrom vertical towers on a balcony to sprawling patches in a backyard. Nothing compares to the taste of a sun-warmed berry picked straight from the plant. They are perennials, meaning a little effort now will reward you for years to come.

How to Grow

01

Soil Preparation

Strawberries love slightly acidic soil (pH 5.5-6.5). Enrich the ground with plenty of organic compost. Good drainage is non-negotiable; wet feet mean rot.

02

Planting Depth

This is critical: Plant so the 'crown' (where stems meet roots) is just above soil level. Buried crowns rot; exposed roots dry out. Find the sweet spot.

03

Spacing & Runners

Plant 12-18 inches apart. They will send out 'runners'. If you want fruit, snip these off so energy goes to berries. If you want more plants, let them root!

04

Mulching & Water

Straw is their namesake! Mulch with straw to keep berries off the dirt. Keep soil consistently moist but avoid wetting the leaves to prevent mildew.
