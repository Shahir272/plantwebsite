Apple Tree

The Orchard Classic

Planting Steps

01

Select Varieties

Most apples are not self-fertile. You need two different varieties that bloom at the same time (e.g., Honeycrisp + Gala) for cross-pollination.

02

Chill Hours

Ensure your chosen variety matches your climate's "chill hours" (hours below 45Â°F). Low-chill varieties are needed for warm climates.

03

Pruning

Pruning is critical. In late winter, remove crossing branches to open up the center of the tree to sunlight and airflow.

04

Thinning Fruit

In early summer, remove excess growing apples so there is only one fruit every 6 inches. This prevents branch breakage and yields larger fruit.
